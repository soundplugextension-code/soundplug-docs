Help & Docs

Beta v0.2  ·  Sound Plug

Sound Plug separates vocals and background music from any browser tab using on-device AI. Nothing is uploaded. Everything runs locally.

Getting started

How it works

01

Open any tab

Navigate to a page with audio playing — a song, video, podcast.

02

Click Sound Plug

The extension captures your tab's audio and loads the AI model on first use.

03

Adjust the sliders

🗣️ controls vocals. 🎶 controls background. Drag either to zero to mute it.

FAQ

Common questions

Sound Plug uses a large AI model (~200MB) that runs entirely in your browser. It downloads once on first use and is cached locally — every session after that is instant.
No. Everything runs on your device. Your audio never leaves your browser. The only external request Sound Plug makes is downloading the AI model from Hugging Face on first install.
AI separation requires processing audio in ~7 second chunks. This creates an inherent latency of a few seconds. This is a property of the model, not a bug.
Sound Plug works on any tab playing audio — YouTube, SoundCloud, Spotify Web, podcasts, and more. Some sites with DRM-protected audio (like Netflix) may not be supported.
The green AI badge appears once the model has fully loaded. If it never appears, your connection may have interrupted the download. Close the popup, reopen it, and try again. If the issue persists, contact support.
Sound Plug uses WebAssembly with SIMD acceleration and works on most modern CPUs. On older machines, model loading may take longer and performance may vary.
